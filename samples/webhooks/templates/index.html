Quip Webhooks

Step 1

Enter your Quip API Access Token
The access token determines what user the messages will be posted as.

Step 2

Choose a thread to post to
Threads are identified by their 11-character ID in the URL: quip.com/ABCDEFghijk.

Step 3

Choose a service
  • Notifications of commits
  • Notifications of issues
  • Notifications of new incidents

Step 4

Get your URL

Step 5

Add the web hook to the service
  1. Go to your repository's settings page.
  2. Choose the "Webhooks & Services" item from the sidebar.
  3. Press the "Add webhook" button.
  4. Paste in the URL in the "Payload URL" field.
  5. From the "Payload version" drop-down, choose application/vnd.github.v3+sjon.
  6. Leave the events radio buttons to the push option.
  7. Press the "Add webhook" button.

If everything works as expected, you'll get a "GitHub Web Hook initialized." message in the thread that you selected.

  1. Go to your app's page.
  2. From the gear menu in the left bar, choose "App".
  3. Choose the integrations tab.
  4. Scroll down to "Web Hook" and select it.
  5. Press the "Activate" button on the right hand side.
  6. Paste in the URL in the single form field.
  7. Press the "Verify" button.

If everything works as expected, you'll get a "Crashlytics Web Hook initialized." message in the thread that you selected.

  1. Go to your services page.
  2. Select the service.
  3. Under "Webhooks", click "Add a webhook".
  4. Enter a name, and paste the URL in the "Endpoint URL" box.
  5. Click "Save" to enable the webhook.
